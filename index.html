About

The very first notes of "be alright" were born in the Normandy.
I was sitting outside in the sun with my guitar, surrounded by stillness. A cat slowly drifted into sleep nearby, while birds filled the air with their songs. In that quiet, peaceful moment, I was reminded of how beautiful life can be.

Having passed through days that felt heavier than others, I’ve come to see how important it is to hold on to those fragments of peace. The ones that whisper that, in the end, everything will fall into place.

The song is written like a letter to a younger version of myself. One who carried doubts and fears of the future. It’s a gentle reminder that time brings healing, that love always finds its way back, and that, eventually, it’s going to be alright.
